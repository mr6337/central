Strawberry DNA Extraction

This Lab is a collaborative and hands-on experiment that enables students to isolate and comprehend the data and DNA we collect from the Fruit. We will be using strawberries for this experiment because they are considered to be an octoploid, which is an organism that has eight homologous pairs of chromosomes, ultimately making the DNA we collect more accessible to isolate. We will be crushing the strawberries to break down the cell walls, combined with a solution of salt and soap, to dissipate the cellular plasma and output DNA within the mixture. This experiment shows the structures of DNA and involves students in the principal techniques of cellular Biology. If you're interested to participate in this Lab save this and text me!

Heart Rate Variability

This Lab will measure the different fluctuations within the time gaps of different heartbeats, which will reflect the Instinctive nervous system control over the functioning heart. The students will be able to measure resting heart rates compared to post-exercise heart rates to understand how different individuals can rejuvenate post physical activities. When comparing these different heart rates, students will be able to acquire understanding into the cardiovascular aspect and the body's capacity to handle stress. This experiment offers various informative elements, not only about Circulatory physiology but also the prominence of assessing the recovery process within health assessments. Osmosis and Diffusion Experiment

This Lab will be observing osmosis in action using potatoes. Students will cut up the potatoes and saturate them within the distilled water, sugar water, and saltwater, so that they can observe how the water articulates across the potato cell membranes. This process happens through osmosis, where the water flows through certain parts of elevated density, leading to alterations in the size and texture of the potato pieces. This hands-on lab offers a better understanding towards the principles of diffusion and osmosis and their impact on cellular activity. Message me if you are interested in engaging in this Lab!
